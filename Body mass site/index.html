What your BMI result means

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 is considered a 'healthy weight'. Maintaining a healthy weight may lower your chances of experiencing health issues on, such as obesity and type 2 diabetes. Aim for a nutritious diet with reduced fat and sugar content, incorporating ample fruits and vegetables. Additionally, strive for regular physical activity, ideally about 30 minutes daily for five days a week.

Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is often a practical indicator of a healthy weight, it is not suited for evry person. Specific groups shoul carefully consider thier BMI outcomes and in a certain cases, the measurement may not be beneficial to use.

Gender

the development and fat body composition of girls and boys vary with age. consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating thier BMI

Age

in aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underesstimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI may misclarify muscular individuals as iverweight or ibese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

pregnant_woman Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-prenancy BMI is advisable to minimize health risks for both mother and child

Race

certain Health concerns may affect individuals of some black and Asian origins at lower BMIs than others. To learn more, it is advised to discuss this with your GP or practice nurse.
